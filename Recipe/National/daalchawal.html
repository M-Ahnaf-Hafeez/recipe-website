Pakistani Daal Chawal

Ingredients

  • For the Daal (Lentil Curry):
    • 1 cup split red lentils (masoor dal)
    • 4 cups water
    • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
    • 2 medium tomatoes (chopped)
    • 1-2 green chilies (slit lengthwise)
    • 1 tsp garlic paste
    • 1 tsp ginger paste
    • 1 tsp turmeric powder
    • 1 tsp red chili powder
    • 1 tsp cumin seeds
    • 1 tsp mustard seeds
    • 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
    • 2-3 tbsp cooking oil or ghee
    • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped, for garnish)
    • Fresh lemon wedges (for serving)
  • For the Chawal (Rice):
    • 2 cups basmati rice
    • 4 cups water
    • 1 tbsp salt
    • 1 tbsp oil or ghee
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 2-3 cloves
    • 1 cinnamon stick

Instructions

  • Preparing the Daal:
    • Rinse the lentils under cold water until the water runs clear.
    • In a large pot, combine lentils and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer until lentils are soft (20-25 minutes). Stir occasionally.
    • In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds, allowing them to splutter.
    • Add chopped onion and fry until golden brown. Stir in garlic paste, ginger paste, and green chilies, cooking for 1-2 minutes.
    • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and chopped tomatoes. Cook until tomatoes are soft and oil begins to separate.
    • Add the cooked lentils to the pan with the onion-tomato mixture. Season with salt and simmer for another 5-10 minutes. Adjust the consistency with water if needed.
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  • Preparing the Rice:
    • Rinse the basmati rice until the water runs clear.
    • In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add salt, oil or ghee, bay leaf, cloves, and cinnamon stick.
    • Add the rinsed rice, reduce heat, cover, and cook until rice is tender and water is absorbed (15-20 minutes). Fluff with a fork.
  • Serving:
    • Serve the hot daal over a bed of rice.
    • Serve with fresh lemon wedges and additional fresh coriander if desired.
