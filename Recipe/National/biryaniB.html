Pakistani-Style Beef Biryani

Ingredients

  • For the Beef Marinade:
    • 1 kg beef (cut into pieces)
    • 1 cup plain yogurt
    • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
    • 2 tbsp biryani masala (store-bought or homemade)
    • 1 tsp red chili powder
    • 1 tsp turmeric powder
    • 1 tsp garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • For the Rice:
    • 3 cups basmati rice
    • 4-5 green cardamom pods
    • 4-5 cloves
    • 2 bay leaves
    • 1 cinnamon stick
    • 1 tsp cumin seeds
    • Salt to taste
    • Water for boiling
  • For the Biryani:
    • 3 large onions (thinly sliced)
    • 3-4 tomatoes (chopped)
    • 2-3 green chilies (sliced)
    • ½ cup fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
    • ½ cup fresh mint leaves (chopped)
    • 1 cup cooking oil or ghee
    • ½ cup milk
    • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
    • 2-3 drops of kewra essence (optional)
    • 2 boiled eggs (halved) for garnish (optional)
    • Fried onions and lemon slices for garnish

Instructions

  • Marinating the Beef:
    • Mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, salt, and lemon juice in a bowl.
    • Coat beef pieces with the mixture. Cover and refrigerate for 2-4 hours or overnight for best results.
  • Preparing the Rice:
    • Wash and soak the basmati rice for 30 minutes.
    • In a large pot, boil water with green cardamom, cloves, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, cumin seeds, and salt.
    • Add the soaked rice and cook until 70-80% done. Drain and set aside.
  • Cooking the Beef:
    • In a large pot, heat oil or ghee and fry the sliced onions until golden brown. Remove half for garnish.
    • Add the marinated beef to the pot and cook on medium heat until the beef is tender and oil separates from the masala. This may take 45-60 minutes.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until tomatoes soften.
  • Layering the Biryani:
    • Spread half the cooked rice over the beef mixture.
    • Sprinkle half the coriander and mint leaves over the rice.
    • Add the remaining rice on top, followed by the remaining coriander and mint leaves.
    • Dissolve saffron in warm milk and pour over the rice, or sprinkle a few drops of kewra essence.
  • Dum (Steam) Cooking:
    • Seal the pot with a tight-fitting lid. Optionally, seal with dough to trap steam.
    • Cook on low heat for 30-40 minutes. You can also place the pot on a tawa to prevent direct heat.
  • Serving:
    • Carefully mix the layers before serving.
    • Garnish with boiled eggs, fried onions, and lemon slices.
    • Enjoy hot with raita or a fresh salad.
