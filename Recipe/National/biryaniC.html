Pakistani-Style Chicken Biryani

Ingredients

  • For the Chicken Marinade:
    • 1 kg chicken (cut into pieces)
    • 1 cup plain yogurt
    • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
    • 2 tbsp biryani masala (store-bought or homemade)
    • 1 tsp red chili powder
    • 1 tsp turmeric powder
    • 1 tsp garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • For the Rice:
    • 3 cups basmati rice
    • 4-5 green cardamom pods
    • 4-5 cloves
    • 2 bay leaves
    • 1 cinnamon stick
    • 1 tsp cumin seeds
    • Salt to taste
    • Water for boiling
  • For the Biryani:
    • 3 large onions (thinly sliced)
    • 3-4 tomatoes (chopped)
    • 2-3 green chilies (sliced)
    • ½ cup fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
    • ½ cup fresh mint leaves (chopped)
    • 1 cup cooking oil or ghee
    • ½ cup milk
    • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
    • 2-3 drops of kewra essence (optional)
    • 2 boiled eggs (halved) for garnish (optional)
    • Fried onions and lemon slices for garnish

Instructions

  • Marinating the Chicken:
    • Mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, salt, and lemon juice in a large bowl.
    • Add the chicken pieces, ensuring they are well coated with the marinade.
    • Cover and refrigerate for at least 1-2 hours or overnight for best results.
  • Preparing the Rice:
    • Wash and soak the basmati rice for 30 minutes.
    • In a large pot, boil water with green cardamom, cloves, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, cumin seeds, and salt.
    • Add the soaked rice and cook until it is 70-80% done. The grains should still be a bit firm.
    • Drain the rice and set aside.
  • Cooking the Chicken:
    • In a large pot, heat oil or ghee and fry the sliced onions until golden brown. Remove half the fried onions for garnish.
    • Add the marinated chicken and cook on medium heat until the chicken is fully cooked and the oil separates from the masala.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and green chilies, and cook until the tomatoes soften.
  • Layering the Biryani:
    • In the same pot with the cooked chicken, spread half of the cooked rice evenly over the chicken.
    • Sprinkle half of the coriander and mint leaves over the rice.
    • Add the remaining rice on top, followed by the remaining coriander and mint leaves.
    • Dissolve saffron strands in warm milk and pour over the rice, or sprinkle a few drops of kewra essence for aroma.
  • Dum (Steam) Cooking:
    • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can seal the lid with dough to trap the steam inside.
    • Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Alternatively, you can place the pot on a tawa (flat griddle) to prevent direct heat from burning the bottom.
  • Serving:
    • Gently mix the biryani layers before serving to combine the flavors.
    • Garnish with boiled eggs, fried onions, and lemon slices.
    • Serve hot with raita (yogurt sauce) or a fresh salad.
